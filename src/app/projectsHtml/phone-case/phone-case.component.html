Phone Case for Google Pixel3

Why buy a $50 phone case when you can print for $1 worth of fillament?

SolidWorks files, STL, and TODO files can be found here

I recently switched cell phone carriers and had to upgrade from my super basic LG K8 to the fancy Google Pixel3. After settling on a payment plan I wasn't about to drop an extra $50 on a phone case. After all I didn't learn CAD and buy/assemble a 3D printer to have it sit there.

I found this great tutorial on desigining your own cell phone cases in SolidWorks and found a great 3D model of the Pixel 3 itself. With this info and honestly a bit of 'eye-balling' about 3 hrs later I had a finalized 3D model of my customized case.

But why stop there?

Recently I've been wanting to make my phone basically a portable terminal for all my network devices (raspberry Pi, Dev Computer, etc). So after about an hour in SolidWorks I had came to this design for a phone holder. Now using a bluetooth keyboard, my Pixel3, and a app called Termius I can SSH into any of my system from anywhere!

O.K. to be honest I'm no GD&T expert, but I know my printer pretty well and again i just kinda 'eyeballed' the dimensions, but once it came off the printer I couldn't believe how well it fit. It really almost snapps together with the case and hold it quite nicely!
